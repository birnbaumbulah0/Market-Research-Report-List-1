<p><p>Scintillation crystals are materials that emit light when exposed to ionizing radiation, making them essential for radiation detection applications. The market is divided into two main types: organic crystals and inorganic crystals. Organic crystals, such as anthracene and stilbene, are characterized by their sensitivity and fast response times but tend to have lower density and atomic number. Inorganic crystals, like sodium iodide and cesium iodide, offer higher detection efficiency and energy resolution, making them suitable for a broader range of applications in nuclear and medical fields.</p></p>
