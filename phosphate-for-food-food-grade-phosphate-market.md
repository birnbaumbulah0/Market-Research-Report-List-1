<p><p>Food-grade phosphates, such as STPP (Sodium Tripolyphosphate), SHMP (Sodium Hexametaphosphate), SAPP (Sodium Acid Pyrophosphate), and TSPP (Tetrasodium Pyrophosphate), are widely used in the food industry for their functional properties. STPP enhances water retention in seafood and meats, while SHMP acts as a sequestrant and emulsifier in dairy and processed foods. SAPP is a leavening agent in baked goods, and TSPP stabilizes and thickens products like cheese and sauces. These phosphates improve texture, shelf life, and overall quality, catering to diverse food applications globally.</p></p>
