<p><p>The Output >300 MW Condensing Steam Turbine market can be divided into two main types: recombustion and non-recombustion systems. Recombustion turbines involve a process where the steam is reheated to increase efficiency and output, optimizing energy extraction. In contrast, non-recombustion turbines operate without reheating the steam, relying on direct expansion for energy production. Each type serves distinct operational needs, with recombustion typically offering higher efficiency for large-scale power generation, while non-recombustion systems may focus on cost-effectiveness and simpler designs.</p></p>
