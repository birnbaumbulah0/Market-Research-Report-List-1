<p><p>The anodized aluminum extrusions market is primarily segmented into two types: AC anodization and DC anodization. AC anodization uses alternating current to create a thicker, more durable oxide layer, enhancing corrosion resistance and aesthetic appeal, making it popular for architectural applications. Conversely, DC anodization employs direct current, resulting in a smoother finish with precise control over coating thickness, ideal for industrial components requiring specific performance characteristics. Both methods enhance aluminum properties, catering to diverse market demands.</p></p>
